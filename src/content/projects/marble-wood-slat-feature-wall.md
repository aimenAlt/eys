This living-room feature wall pairs a tall marble-look tile center panel with dark vertical wood-slat accents on both sides. The scope was a floor-to-ceiling composition: tile layout and setting, slat carpentry, and clean edges where the new wall meets existing paint and trim.

Feature walls like this succeed when center and side elements share one vertical plane and finished height. We plan tile coursing so seams stay balanced, then build the wood-slat wings so spacing, reveal, and color read as intentional framing. Transitions at the ceiling, base, and side walls get special attention so the wall looks built-in, not stuck on.

Materials combined marble-look tile for the bright center with stained vertical slats for contrast. [Custom carpentry](/services/custom-carpentry/) handled the slat assemblies and edge details; tile was sequenced so the center panel stayed square and plumb against the wood. The finished surface is ready for furniture and, when desired, a centered TV—common next steps for homeowners browsing our [TV and media walls](/our-work/?category=tv-media-walls) gallery.

The outcome is a strong focal wall that anchors the living room without a full remodel. Light hits the tile and slats differently through the day, so the wall has depth even when the room is quiet. Mixed-material accents are a frequent request for open-concept great rooms across Katy and West Houston.
