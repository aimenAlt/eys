This project covered floor-to-ceiling curtain and drapery hardware in a two-story living space—tall openings, long panels, and mounting that stays level across a high window wall. The goal was soft, even sheers that read as one continuous treatment from the floor up, not rods that sag or panels that hang short.

High-ceiling [curtain installation](/services/curtain-installation/) starts with solid backing and hardware that can span without droop. We measure height and width, set brackets at consistent elevations, and confirm the rod line before hanging fabric. On double-height walls, that means stable ladders or lifts, fasteners into studs or proper anchors, and checking level across long runs so the eye does not catch a tilt from the floor.

Sheer white panels were hung to pool cleanly and stack neatly when open. Adjacent bedroom windows in the same visit kept hardware style coordinated through the home. Where the window wall curves, panel spacing and rod segments were adjusted so the drapery follows the architecture.

The finished look softens the tall glass, improves privacy and light control, and gives the living room a complete appearance next to existing lighting. Many West Houston homes with two-story great rooms need this high-reach approach rather than retail rods meant for eight-foot walls.
