This home-office project combined a floor-to-ceiling navy built-in wall with white French doors, sidelights, and fluted casing. The scope covered open shelving above lower cabinets, a painted finish with hardware, and a door assembly that frames the office as a distinct room while keeping light from the adjacent space.

Built-ins were laid out for storage below and display above, with consistent shelf spacing and cabinet reveals so the wall reads as one unit. Cabinetry and shelving were installed plumb and level, then finished in a deep navy that contrasts the white door and trim package. Hardware pulls completed the furniture-like look without overcomplicating the face.

On the entry side, French doors with sidelights and a transom were assembled and trimmed with decorative fluted casing and corner blocks. That trim work ties the doorway to the office finish and is the kind of detail that sits at the overlap of [custom carpentry](/services/custom-carpentry/) and [door repair and installation](/services/door-repair-installation/). Alignment, swing, and casing miters matter as much as paint color when the doors become the visual frame for a bold built-in wall.

The result is a finished office that feels intentional: storage where it is needed, open shelves for books and décor, and a bright white doorway that balances the navy millwork.
