- Homeowners replacing or adding cabinets in baths and kitchens
- New-build buyers upgrading builder-grade cabinets with quality pulls and knobs
- Families adding pantry, laundry, or garage storage cabinetry after move-in
- Anyone who wants hardware perfectly aligned — not “close enough”

From production homes in Fulshear to established Katy floor plans, cabinet work succeeds when measuring, leveling, and fastening are done with patience. ## What we focus on during install

Secure installation, consistent reveals, and hardware layouts that look intentional. We use careful measuring and drilling practices to protect veneers and doors. Soft-close adjustments, filler strips, scribe work, and trim details are part of making the install look finished rather than unfinished. Complex custom cabinet packages may involve specialty fabrication partners; we clarify that early so expectations stay honest.

A typical visit includes confirming wall conditions and stud locations, protecting floors and counters, hanging and leveling units, adjusting doors and drawers, installing hardware with consistent spacing, and leaving the space clean. Plumbing or countertop coordination is called out in the estimate when it affects sequencing. If you already purchased cabinets from a big-box or specialty retailer, bring model numbers and delivery timing so we can plan the install around your schedule.
